Benjamin Arthur McKee (born April 7, 1985) is an American musician, songwriter, and record producer best known as the bassist for the Grammy Award-winning rock band Imagine Dragons. Raised in Forestville, California, McKee grew up playing acoustic guitar and violin before specializing in the bass, eventually attending the Berklee College of Music. In 2009, he was recruited by guitarist Wayne Sermon to join the band, subsequently dropping out of his final semester to move to Las Vegas and complete the group's lineup alongside drummer Daniel Platzman. Beyond his musical contributions to multi-platinum albums like Night Visions and Evolve, McKee is recognized for his extensive philanthropy, including his involvement with the Tyler Robinson Foundation and a personal 2021 initiative where he donated $1,000 a day to various charities for an entire year.
