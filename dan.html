Daniel Coulter Reynolds, born July 14, 1987, in Las Vegas, Nevada, is a celebrated American singer and songwriter best known as the lead vocalist of the Grammy-winning band Imagine Dragons. Standing at 6'4" (193 cm), he is recognized for his powerful stage presence and a career that began after he formed the band while studying at Brigham Young University. Beyond global hits like "Radioactive" and "Believer," Reynolds is a prominent advocate for mental health and LGBTQ+ rights, having founded the LOVELOUD Festival in 2017 to support at-risk youth. In his personal life, he is a father of four and has been remarkably open about his struggles with Ankylosing Spondylitis and Ulcerative Colitis, using his platform to help destigmatize chronic illness and depression.
