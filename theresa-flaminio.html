Theresa Flaminio, known professionally as DANiiVORY, is an American singer, songwriter, and multi-instrumentalist who served as the keyboardist and backing vocalist for Imagine Dragons from August 2011 to early 2012. A graduate of the Berklee College of Music, she joined the band alongside drummer Daniel Platzman following the departure of the Tolmans and performed during their 2011 West Coast tour. While her tenure with the group was brief and ended before the release of their major-label debut, she went on to achieve significant success as a touring musician for high-profile artists, including Beyoncé—performing at the 2013 Super Bowl halftime show—as well as CeeLo Green, 2NE1, and Bridgit Mendler. In addition to her work as a session and touring musician, she has pursued a solo career, releasing dance-pop and R&B-influenced music under her stage name and appearing as a musical director on the reality series Sisterhood of Hip Hop.
